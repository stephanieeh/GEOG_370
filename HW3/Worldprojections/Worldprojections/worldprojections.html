In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS:

I displayed the maps in different projections by searching for the projection number in the Projection Coordinate Reference System in QGIS. Then, I ran Indicatrix on that projection to develop the distortion circles and the longitude/latitude lines. From there, I tried out different projections and made new print layouts with each projection.

WGS84 Projection

Shape: preserved for the most part, especially along the equator, flattened closer to the poles.

Area: preserved along the equator and the prime meridian, area increases as we get closer to the poles.

Distance: I think distance is preserved, but not sure.

Direction: distorted? possibly because it is a rectangle.

Aitoff Projection

Shape: preserved along the equator and the prime meridian, distorted towards the poles

Area: preserved for the most part, distorted towards the NE, SE, NW, and SW corners of the map

Distance: looks distorted towards the SE, NE, SW, NW corners.

Direction: presumably preserves because it is a modified azimuthal projection

Pseudo-Mercator

Shape: preserves shape completely.

Area: preserves for the most part, heavily distorts to be greater along the poles.

Distance: distance appears greater towards the poles.

Direction: distorted? possibly because it is a rectangle.

Winkel Sphere

Shape: preserves along the equator and the prime meridian, distorts near the NE, SE, NW, SW corners of the map.

Area: preserves along the equator and the prime meridian, distorts near the poles.

Distance: I think distance is preserved, but not sure.

Direction: presumably preserves because it is a modified azimuthal projection.

World Cylindrical Equal Area

Shape: preserves for the most part, heavily distorted along the poles.

Area: preserves for the most part, heavily distorted along the poles.

Distance: I think distance is preserved, but not sure.

Direction: distorted? possibly because it is a rectangle.

World Equidistant Conic

Shape: distortion increases as we move away from the North Pole.

Area: distortion increases as we move away from the North Pole.

Distance: presumably preserved because it is an equidistant projection.

Direction: not distorted? because it is a circle? not sure.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

Shape: distortion increases as we move away from the North Pole.

Area: distortion increases as we move away from the North Pole; more distorted than the World Equidistant Conic.

Distance: presumably preserved because it is an azimuthal projection.

Direction: presumably preserved because it is an equidistant projection.

Peirce Quincuncial North Pole Diamond

Shape: completely preserved.

Area: preserved for the most part, some areas where size is increased.

Distance: I think it is distorted but I'm not sure.

Direction: uncertain.

World Quartic Authalic

Shape: preserved along the equator and the prime meridian, distortion increases in NE, SE, NW, SW corners.

Area: preserved for the most part, distortion increases towards the poles.

Distance: appears preserved, uncertain.

Direction: possibly preserved, uncertain.
